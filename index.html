About

Kolton is a Computer Science student at the University of Missouri. His research interests include computer vision and autonomous robotics with a particular emphasis on autonomous vehicles. He is the Head Electrical Engineer for the hydrogen car division of Mizzou Eco Racing.

TigerDrive

TigerDrive is the University of Missouri's first autonomous vehicle build project. It is entirely student led, developed, and maintained. The vehicle is a 1997 Chevy S-10 that has been converted to an electric vehicle by Mizzou Eco Racing. The vehicle is currently being converted to an autonomous vehicle by Kolton and the rest of the Mizzou Eco Racing team.

Slither Bot

This project is a bot for the popular online game Slither.io. The bot uses a convolutional nerual network to make navigational choices in a multi-agent environment. Kolton is currently attempting to learn the game with reinforcment learning as an educational effort.

Teaching

Kolton has served as a teaching assistant for two semesters in the University of Missouri course CS1050 and for one semester in the University of Missouri course CS2050. These classes are introductory Computer Science courses for Computer Science majors. They are taught using the C programming language. CS1050 is predominantly focused on C syntax and general programming concepts. CS2050 uses the C language to implement basic data structures such as Stacks, Queues, and Binary Trees. Descriptions of the courses can be found here in the University of Missouri course catalog.
